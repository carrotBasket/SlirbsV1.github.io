Setup

Assumptions: Game uses a square grid board. "Slirb cards" define units/items and track HP.

  • Unfold the board. Separate all Green and Brown "Slirb" unit/item cards into two distinct decks.
  • Give each player their team's deck faced down. Each player draws 3 cards. If you do not draw a Slirb unit card, reshuffle your hand into the deck and draw 3 new cards until one is present.
  • Each player receives 11 pawns. Place all 11 pawns anywhere within your designated "Base Zone" (e.g., the two back rows of the board).
  • Each player takes a piece of paper to track their team's resources (Start: 50 Money, 10 Energy) and the Health Points (HP) of their active Slirb units.

Gameplay

The youngest player gets the first turn.

  • Movement/Capture Phase: Move up to 3 different pawns 1 square (orthogonally or diagonally) in any direction to capture an empty square. Alternately, place one new pawn from your supply onto a controlled square.
  • Attack Phase (Optional): If an enemy pawn is within 5 squares (radius) of your pawn, you may declare an attack.
  • Battle Sequence:
    • Both players reveal the Slirb unit card associated with their battling pawn.
    • Track HP on your paper. Choose an attack action listed on your Slirb card, pay the required Money/Energy, and apply the damage to the opponent's Slirb HP. Your opponent does the same (simultaneously or sequentially as agreed).
    • When a Slirb runs out of health, the loser discards that card and removes the pawn from the board. The winner keeps their card/pawn active.
  • Turn End: After all actions (movement/attack) are complete, draw one card from your deck. Your turn is over.
  • Victory Condition: The first player to successfully move one of their pawns onto an opponent's "Base Zone" wins the game instantly.

Special Rules

Specific abilities are tied to unit/item cards. Assume abilities are clearly defined on their respective cards.

  • Tank / Artillery: These units cannot initiate attacks against unengaged enemy units. They can only join existing battles initiated by other units to provide supporting fire.
  • Barriers: Barriers are placed using specific item cards. Pawns cannot be attacked behind a barrier by most units.
  • Battering Ram / Sniper: The Battering Ram item card is required to destroy a Barrier pawn. The Sniper unit card is the only unit that can bypass the protection of a Barrier and attack a pawn behind it.
